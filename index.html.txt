<h2>Introduction</h2>

<p>
    Welcome to Math 51 Prep! My name is Galilea Gonzalez, and I am a third-year
    UC Berkeley student studying aerospace engineering and mathematics. I will
    be your learning facilitator for Math 51. Over the next couple of weeks, my
    goal is to introduce you to the material you will see in Math 51 up until
    your first midterm, so you can get a head start before taking the course in
    the fall or spring.
</p>

<h2>Class Overview</h2>

<p>
    Before each lecture, I will post my lecture notes in case you would like to
    get ahead or follow along during class. During lecture, we will go over the
    material scheduled for that day, work through examples, and practice problems
    together. In addition to lecture, there will be discussion sections where a
    tutor and I will be available to help. During these sections, I will provide
    homework that you should complete during class. Please use this time to ask
    questions and get support.
</p>

<p>
    I will also provide additional resources to help you succeed, not only during
    this prep course but also when you take Math 51 during the academic year. For
    each section we learn, I will make a one-page study guide that you can use as
    a quick reference while studying. One thing I learned from taking
    lower-division math classes is that having everything organized on one page
    can make it much easier to understand the main ideas. I will also link
    helpful YouTube videos from channels that explain the material clearly, as
    well as past exams and review resources that may be useful when you take the
    full course.
</p>
<h2>Textbook Resource</h2>

<p>
    A textbook PDF/link is provided below so you can use it as a reference
    throughout the prep course. This is the book that every professor uses for
    Math 51, so you have it available here on this website. I recommend
    downloading it so you have it for the future.
</p>

<p>
    During this prep program, you are not required to read the textbook before
    lecture. The main goal is for you to be introduced to the topics and get
    practice with the material.
</p>

<p>
    However, when you take Math 51 during the academic year, I strongly
    recommend reading the textbook section before lecture. Even if you do not
    understand everything right away, reading ahead can help you recognize the
    main ideas, definitions, and examples before your professor explains them
    in class.
</p>
