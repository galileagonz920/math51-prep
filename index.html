Math 51 Prep: Calculus I

Introduction

Welcome to Math 51 Prep! My name is Galilea Gonzalez, and I am a third-year UC Berkeley student studying Aerospace Engineering and Mathematics. I will be your learning facilitator for Math 51. Over the next couple of weeks, my goal is to introduce you to the material you will see in Math 51 up until your first midterm, so you can get a head start before taking the course in the fall or spring.

Class Overview

Lectures will take place from 9:00 AM to 10:30 AM in 164 and 166 Grimes Engineering Center. Before each lecture, I will post my lecture notes in case you would like to get ahead or follow along during class. During lecture, we will go over the material scheduled for that day, work through examples, and practice problems together.

In addition to lecture, there will be discussion sections where a tutor and I will be available to help. During these sections, I will provide homework that you should complete during class. Please use this time to ask questions, work with others, and get support on the material.

I will also provide additional resources to help you succeed, not only during this prep course but also when you take Math 51 during the academic year. For each section we learn, I will make a one-page study guide that you can use as a quick reference while studying. One thing I learned from taking lower-division math classes is that having everything organized on one page can make it much easier to understand the main ideas. I will also link helpful YouTube videos from channels that explain the material clearly, as well as past exams and review resources that may be useful when you take the full course.
